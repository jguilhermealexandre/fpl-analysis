üõ°Ô∏è Selecting Defenders & Goalkeepers

The Golden Rule: 80% of defensive FPL points come from clean sheets. A ¬£4.5m defender from a top team with easy fixtures will outscore a ¬£7.0m attacking wingback with tough fixtures.

What to look for:

  • Clean Sheet Probability: Target teams with 40%+ probability. This metric combines defensive form (goals conceded) with upcoming fixture difficulty.
  • Defender Rating: 65+ is excellent. This weighs clean sheet % heavily (the most important factor) plus fixture difficulty.
  • Goals Conceded/Game: Under 1.0 is elite, under 1.5 is good. Lower = more clean sheets = more points.
  • Home Fixtures: Teams keep 15% more clean sheets at home. A team with 3+ home games in next 5 is gold for defenders.

Strategy Example: Liverpool conceding 0.6 goals/game with 3 home fixtures and 55% CS probability? Stack their defense. Ignore expensive attacking defenders from teams with poor defensive records and tough fixtures.

‚öîÔ∏è Selecting Attackers (Midfielders & Forwards)

The Key Insight: Fixtures matter less for attackers (35-40% points variance) than defenders (50-60%), but they still matter! Premium attackers can return against anyone, but budget options NEED good fixtures.

What to look for:

  • Attacker Rating: 65+ means the team is scoring goals AND has weak opponents coming up. Perfect for picking their midfielders/forwards.
  • Goals Scored/Game: 2.0+ is excellent attacking form. These teams create chances.
  • Avg FDR (Fixture Difficulty): Under 3.0 is good. Under 2.5 is excellent. This means they're facing defensively weak teams.
  • Home Fixtures: Teams score 0.4-0.6 more goals per game at home. Attackers from teams with 3+ home games are boosted.

Strategy Example: Man City scoring 2.5 goals/game with FDR of 2.3 and 4 home fixtures? Their attackers become captain candidates. Avoid attackers from low-scoring teams (under 1.2 goals/game) facing tough fixtures (FDR 4+).

üìÖ Understanding Fixture Swings (The Secret Weapon)

What Elite FPL Managers Do: They don't react to last week's haul. They plan 2-3 gameweeks ahead, buying players BEFORE their easy fixture run starts.

Why This Works:

  • Player prices rise when everyone sees the good fixtures
  • Template ownership increases, reducing your differential advantage
  • By the time fixtures turn green, it's too late - you've missed value gains

How to Use Fixture Trend:

  • "Getting Easier": Buy NOW if the team has good form. You're 2 weeks early = perfect timing.
  • "Stable": Safe hold. No urgency to buy or sell based on fixtures alone.
  • "Getting Harder": Plan your exit strategy. Don't wait until GW1 of the tough run.

Pro Tip: The Fixture Swings tab shows "Prime Targets" - teams with easy fixtures getting even easier. These are your highest priority transfers. The edge comes from acting while they're still "yellow" on other tools.

üè† Home vs Away: Why It Matters

The Data: Home teams score 0.4-0.6 more goals per game AND keep clean sheets 15% more often. This is huge!

How to Use Home/Away Split:

  • 3+ Home Fixtures: Major boost for both attackers AND defenders from that team
  • 3+ Away Fixtures: Still okay for premium attackers, but avoid budget defenders unless team has elite defense
  • Balanced (2H/3A or 3H/2A): Neutral - rely on other metrics

Real Example: Brighton at home vs Brighton away are two different teams. If they have 4 home games coming up, their defenders become much more attractive (even if overall FDR is medium).

üìä How to Read the Metrics

Form Score (0-100): Composite metric combining goals scored, goals conceded, wins, clean sheets, and upcoming fixtures. 70+ is excellent form, 50-69 is good, under 50 needs monitoring.

FDR - Fixture Difficulty Rating (1-5):

  • 1-2 (Green): Easy fixtures - prime time to own assets from this team
  • 3 (Yellow): Medium - okay for premiums, avoid budget options
  • 4-5 (Red): Tough fixtures - only keep proven premium assets, sell budget picks

Clean Sheet Probability: Our algorithm combines: defensive record (60% weight), fixture difficulty (30%), and home advantage (10%). 50%+ is excellent, 30-50% is good, under 30% means avoid defenders.

Attack Rating & Defense Rating (0-100): Position-specific scores. 65+ = target, 45-65 = monitor, under 45 = avoid. These tell you specifically whether to pick attackers OR defenders from a team.

  • ‚ùå Not checking home/away split: "Easy fixtures" means different things for home vs away games. 4 away games to "weak" teams is still risky.
